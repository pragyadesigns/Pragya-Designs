**Company:** Enterprise software client (confidential)
**Role:** Product Designer

**Tagline:**
Migrating a large enterprise's partner resource ecosystem to a modular, low-code platform — with a refreshed UI and sharper information architecture that helps partners find what they need, faster.

---

## The problem

A major enterprise software company runs an ecosystem of partner-facing websites — a resource hub where their partner companies (resellers, consultants, and solution providers) access sales pitches, training, funded customer engagements, and marketing materials to help them sell and deploy the client's AI and productivity products to their own customers.

The sites had been built and maintained by an external vendor. The client's internal org wanted full ownership of the websites on a backend that was low-code and maintainable in-house. They also wanted to use the migration as a chance to audit their inventory of partner resources (decks, PDFs, links) and retire stale content in favor of newer, more relevant material.

---

## The task

- Create a refreshed, consistent UI — a standardized look and feel across all pages.
- Design a more intuitive information architecture so partners can find resources faster.
- Rebuild the platform on a modular, maintainable, low-code solution.

---

## Design considerations

> **Visual layout note for the four points below:** Group them into a tabs or accordion component so only one is visible at a time. Leave ample real estate for the visuals. Final choice of pattern is up to you.

### Compact layouts
Users don't scroll much, so everything had to earn its vertical real estate.
#### Components were designed to be compact.
#### Home pages carried minimal information — they served as launch points into deeper pages.
#### Deeper product pages used accordions to collapse sections; only one section opens at a time.

### Visual weight for resources
#### Introduced file-type icons and file-size labels so users know what to expect before clicking.
#### Resources were displayed differently depending on placement — featured resources, accordion items, or resource library entries each had their own treatment.

### Surfacing critical information
#### CTAs pulled into hero sections.
#### Partner updates promoted to the top of home pages.

### Accessibility
#### Ensured adherence to the client's accessibility guidelines throughout.
